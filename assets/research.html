About my Research

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Neurobiology, working the the lab of Sandeep Robert Datta. Before working with Bob, I earned my doctorate in Bernardo Sabatini's lab, where I studied neuromodulation and synaptic calcium signaling in the dendrites and dendritic spines of hippocampal neurons. And before *that*, I studied biochemistry and computer science at the University of Kansas. My long-term research goals are to use electrophysiological and imaging approaches to understanding the circuit and cellular properties of the mammalan olfactory system.

More broadly, I am interested in sensory neurophysiology, emergence in biological other natural systems, algorithms, computer programming and data visualization. This is an admittedly broad list- and indeed, I find myself interested by a wide variety of things. I have a lot less time than I'd like to explore some of these side projects, but as they pop up, I'll be putting them here or on the blog.
